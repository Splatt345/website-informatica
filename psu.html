Power Supply Unit (PSU)

The heart that powers your computer

What is a PSU?

The Power Supply Unit (PSU) is a critical component that converts alternating current (AC) power from your wall outlet into direct current (DC) power that your computer components can use. It's responsible for providing stable, clean power to every component in your system, making it essential for system stability and longevity.

How PSUs Work

PSUs perform several important functions:

  1. AC to DC Conversion: Converts 110-240V AC to multiple DC voltages (3.3V, 5V, 12V, -12V)
  2. Voltage Regulation: Maintains stable voltages despite load fluctuations
  3. Power Distribution: Routes appropriate voltages to different components
  4. Protection: Safeguards components from power issues

Power Ratings and Efficiency

Wattage Rating

The maximum power the PSU can deliver, typically ranging from 450W to 1500W+. Higher wattage isn't always better - it should match your system's needs.

Typical Wattage Needs:

  • Basic Office: 350-450W
  • Gaming PC: 650-850W
  • High-End Gaming: 850-1200W
  • Workstation: 1000-1500W+

80 Plus Efficiency Ratings

Certification that indicates how efficiently the PSU converts AC to DC power. Higher ratings mean less wasted energy and heat.

80 Plus (Standard)

80% efficiency at 20%, 50%, and 100% load

80 Plus Bronze

82-85% efficiency, good for budget builds

80 Plus Gold

87-90% efficiency, popular choice

80 Plus Platinum

90-92% efficiency, premium choice

80 Plus Titanium

92-94% efficiency, highest efficiency

PSU Form Factors

ATX (Standard)

Most common form factor, fits in most desktop cases. Typically 150x86x140mm.

SFX

Smaller form factor for compact cases. 125x100x63.5mm.

TFX

Thin form factor for slim cases. 175x85x65mm.

PSU-C (Server)

Server-grade PSUs with higher reliability and redundancy features.

Cable Types

Non-Modular

All cables are permanently attached. Cheaper but can make cable management difficult.

Semi-Modular

Essential cables are attached, optional cables are modular. Good balance of cost and convenience.

Fully Modular

All cables are detachable. Best for cable management and airflow, but most expensive.

Protection Features

OVP (Over Voltage Protection)

Shuts down PSU if voltages exceed safe levels.

UVP (Under Voltage Protection)

Shuts down PSU if voltages drop too low.

SCP (Short Circuit Protection)

Protects against short circuits in components.

OPP (Over Power Protection)

Shuts down if power draw exceeds rating.

OCP (Over Current Protection)

Protects against excessive current draw.

Power Connectors

24-pin Motherboard

Main power connector for the motherboard.

8-pin CPU

Power for the CPU (sometimes 4+4 pin).

6+2 pin PCIe

Power for graphics cards and expansion cards.

SATA Power

For SATA drives (SSD/HDD).

Molex

Legacy connector for older devices.

Quick Facts

  • PSU quality affects system stability
  • Never cheap out on the PSU
  • Higher efficiency = less heat
  • Modular PSUs improve airflow

Choosing a PSU

  • Calculate total power needs
  • Add 20-30% headroom
  • Choose 80 Plus Gold minimum
  • Check connector requirements
  • Consider case compatibility

Power Calculation

Typical component power draw:

  • CPU: 65-250W
  • GPU: 150-450W
  • Motherboard: 25-100W
  • Storage: 5-15W each
  • Fans: 2-5W each
